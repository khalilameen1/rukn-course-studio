# 03 CAPABILITY MAP AND COURSE ARCHITECTURE

## Backward design

Begin with the end performance and work backward

1. What must the learner deliver or perform
2. What decisions create a good result
3. What execution skills implement those decisions
4. What quality signals expose weakness
5. What failures are common and how are they diagnosed
6. What foundations make those judgements possible
7. What practice sequence builds independence

Do not begin with a table of contents from a book a tool menu or popular video titles

Take the accepted `COURSE_PERFORMANCE_COMPOSITION` as an architecture input
If it is absent provisional or still controlled by a title or broad family label reopen thesis and composition before mapping modules

## Real performance episode and workflow trace

For every course build `PERFORMANCE_EPISODE_MAP` before grouping capabilities into modules
Also maintain `REAL_WORKFLOW_TRACE` as its ordered stage and compatibility view
The workflow object name is broader than employment workflow
Together they trace the promised real performance episode from the first authentic trigger input stimulus request or condition through completion verification likely failure correction escalation referral delivery or handoff

The episode may be a client task live conversation service call teaching session interpretation case decision process production sequence system operation physical action or another real use pattern
Do not force business language onto a course whose authentic sequence is not a business workflow
Do not trace only the central technique or concept
Include the ordinary perception interaction setup documentation correction and completion actions that make the promised performance executable

A required stage may be small but it cannot disappear
If the learner must perform it during the promised real episode the course must teach the action perception decision language signal evidence and recovery before that point

Commonly omitted stages include

- Recognising the incoming signal intent category error or starting state
- Asking for missing information context assets access or permission
- Retrieving the needed knowledge or phrase without a visible answer
- Choosing between plausible routes rather than repeating one memorised response
- Preparing tools environments materials naming structures data or files
- Modelling or demonstrating before asking another person to perform
- Listening observing or checking the counterpart response
- Diagnosing misunderstanding objection weak quality or system failure
- Correcting adapting retrying or escalating
- Documenting the decisive information or resulting state
- Confirming completion understanding acceptance or transfer
- Managing versions approvals exports delivery referral or handoff when relevant

For job oriented or client facing promises also include scope access feedback revision approval delivery and recovery where required
For language or communication promises include listening turn taking clarification register variation adaptation and closure
For teaching promises include learner starting state modelling elicitation error diagnosis correction second attempt and transfer check
For knowledge promises include retrieval distinction misconception repair application boundary and authority limits
For physical promises include safety setup observation correction changed conditions and stop rules

Include acquisition pricing contracting invoicing or ongoing support only when the target role and promise require them
Do not bloat a narrow course with unrelated business teaching
Do not omit a required interaction documentation or escalation step merely because it is not the central subject

## Coverage matrix

Build a matrix before accepting the map

Rows are required capabilities
Columns are learning functions selected from the active adapter

- Introduced in authentic context
- Explained or made conceptually clear
- Demonstrated or modelled
- Perceived discriminated or retrieved where required
- Practised in controlled conditions
- Corrected after a visible error misunderstanding or weak result
- Varied or transferred to a changed case
- Proved through practice or a project
- Reused and integrated
- Completed documented escalated referred delivered or handed off where required

Not every capability needs every column
Its active adapter defines the mandatory route
A topic mention counts as none of these unless it fulfils the function
The matrix must show a path from exposure to independent performance


## Workflow coverage matrix

Build `WORKFLOW_COVERAGE_MATRIX` beside the capability matrix
Rows are required real performance stages and actions
Columns show whether the learner is prepared to continue without a hidden perception decision phrase action check correction or completion step

- Encountered in a realistic performance episode
- Explained before first use
- Demonstrated or modelled
- Perceived listened to or retrieved where required
- Practised
- Proved through a compact practice or project output when progression depends on it
- Supported by a pre existing supplied asset when needed and already available before generation
- Diagnosed when the normal path fails
- Corrected retried or escalated
- Reused under changed input feedback counterpart constraint or context
- Completed documented communicated referred delivered or handed off appropriately

A performance or workflow stage does not count as covered because a lesson mentions its name
Record the exact lesson practice project or pre existing asset that makes the learner able to perform it
Every required row must have a complete route or an explicit out of scope decision that also narrows the promise

## Course spine

Choose a spine that matches the skill

Possible spines include

- One artefact evolving from rough to professional
- One workflow repeated with increasing complexity
- A sequence of real cases with new constraints
- A role simulation from first request through feedback correction delivery and handoff
- A service case moving from intake and classification through response documentation escalation and closure
- A language performance moving from controlled to spontaneous adaptive use
- A teaching session moving from model to elicitation diagnosis correction and transfer
- A system built incrementally then debugged and deployed
- A diagnostic framework applied to increasingly ambiguous evidence

Prefer a spine that makes progress visible and creates usable outputs
Do not create a sequence that feels like unrelated tips

## Invisible episodic progression map

Treat short drama as a structural analogy only
Do not turn the course into a fictional story a performance show or a repeated content formula

Build `EPISODIC_PROGRESSION_MAP` from the capability graph and course spine

```yaml
episodic_progression:
  opening_dislocation:
  capability_accumulation: []
  module_turns: []
  module_bridges: []
  project_proofs: []
  final_boundary_readiness_project:
  earned_peak:
  final_resolution:
  lesson_links:
    - lesson_id:
      real_tension:
      resolved_now:
      learner_payoff:
      retained_capability:
      newly_exposed_problem:
      why_next_is_needed:
      escalation_type:
```

`real_tension` comes from the work itself

- Two plausible choices with different consequences
- A result that looks acceptable but fails inspection
- A constraint that reverses the obvious answer
- Missing information that changes the decision
- A failure whose cause is not where the learner expects
- A capability that works in one context but breaks in another
- Increasing independence ambiguity accountability or integration

Every lesson must pay off its own central question before creating continuation
The next need must be earned by the consequence limit or application of what was just learned
Never leave the current answer incomplete merely to preserve retention

The dramatic curve is capability pressure not performance intensity
Escalate through harder judgement wider integration more realistic constraints less guidance and stronger evidence requirements
Do not escalate through louder language larger claims faster editing fake danger or constant surprise

The progression should disappear from the spoken surface
If the learner can hear the content scaffolding repeatedly the structure is too explicit
If adjacent lessons that claim a dependency can be swapped without changing learning causality inspect the link and reorder merge or rewrite the map
Some capabilities are genuinely parallel
When that is true order them by cognitive load reuse and project need and do not invent a false dependency or cliffhanger

Do not force one beat sheet across domains
A language course may escalate spontaneity
A programming course may escalate system interaction and debugging uncertainty
A sales course may escalate objection ambiguity live pressure qualification follow up and handoff
A call handling course may escalate listening variation case ambiguity emotional pressure tool state and escalation
A teaching course may escalate learner variation hidden misunderstanding correction choice and transfer
A perceptual capability may escalate closer alternatives weaker cues noise and changed conditions
A production course may escalate judgement integration and delivery constraints

## Final boundary readiness contract

For every course with at least two modules designate the project immediately before the final module as `FINAL_INTEGRATED_READINESS`
This is the final formal upload evidence but not a large capstone
It should reuse prior learner work or one bounded supplied case so the learner can integrate without rebuilding an entire professional assignment

```yaml
final_boundary_readiness:
  project_id:
  preceding_module_id:
  final_module_id:
  bounded_real_performance_episode:
  prior_outputs_reused: []
  core_capabilities_represented: []
  promise_critical_capabilities_represented: []
  promise_critical_capabilities_omitted: []
  active_adapters_represented: []
  decisive_integrated_result:
  supported_attachment_set: []
  attachment_self_evidence_check_id:
  expected_active_minutes:
  final_module_actions_using_this_work: []
  final_module_new_promise_critical_capability: false
  readiness_status: ACCEPTED | REVISE_ARCHITECTURE
```

`promise_critical_capabilities_omitted` must be empty
The project should represent most core capabilities and every capability whose absence would make the promised readiness claim false
It may omit low risk supporting knowledge already proved elsewhere when the integrated result does not depend on reproducing it

Keep the task inside the standard compact effort and attachment limits whenever possible
Use accumulated prior work a supplied scenario or a short role simulation rather than a new full scale client assignment
If honest integration cannot fit the compact boundary redesign module order reduce duplicated work split earlier practice more intelligently or narrow the promise
Do not solve the problem by adding a project after the final module

All first introductions of promise critical actions decisions perceptions phrases corrections documentation completion or handoff steps must occur before this boundary
The final module may deepen judgement expose harder variations diagnose the submitted work model refinement reuse known correction loops transfer the performance package it for real use or resolve the professional standard
It must not contain the first teaching of a standalone capability that the learner would need to prove in another upload
It must add completion value and must not exist as a recap only module

## Module bridge close

Build `MODULE_CLOSURE_LEDGER` after module order and project placement are stable

```yaml
- module_bridge:
  module_id:
  module_payoff:
  capability_now_owned:
  real_limit_now_visible:
  next_work_problem:
  project_id:
  placement: after_project
  close_intent:
  spoken_close:
  forbidden_teaser_language: []
  next_module_opening_payoff:
  final_module: false
```

Every non final module boundary ends with the bridge after the spoken project instruction
The bridge is not a separate heading and must not sound like an announcement
It is the last spoken paragraph before the next module heading
It completes the current capability then lets its consequence limitation or new condition make the next capability necessary

Valid bridge sources include

- The completed execution now needs inspection
- The normal case now meets a variation or exception
- A correct component now creates an integration problem
- A controlled result now faces real client feedback or changed constraints
- A working process now needs diagnosis recovery delivery or handoff
- The learner can now see why a previously invisible decision matters

The bridge must not

- Say in the next module next part coming up wait for what follows or an equivalent phrase
- Ask a suspense question whose answer was withheld from the current module
- Invent danger urgency mystery or drama
- Promise a reveal
- Summarise the whole module as a substitute for a close
- Introduce a topic with no causal dependency
- Require the inter module project to use an untaught capability

The final reel completes its own teaching before the project
The project then proves the completed capability under a compact condition
The bridge follows the project and the next module opens from the exposed limit without quoting or replaying the close

The final module has no artificial forward bridge and no project after it
The accepted final boundary project already supplies the compact integrated readiness evidence
The final module uses that integrated work to diagnose refine transfer package communicate deliver hand off or close the promised performance through a final resolution
It does not introduce a new promise critical standalone capability
All formal upload projects remain between modules

Vary bridge logic and sentence shape across the course
Do not reuse one closing formula at every module boundary

## Progression curve

Build an intentional curve

### Orientation through consequence

Reveal how the field actually works and what controls quality
Give the learner a useful action early
Do not spend the opening on greetings biography course logistics or generic motivation

### Foundation in use

Teach the smallest foundations needed to execute a meaningful task
Avoid encyclopaedic preliminaries

### Controlled execution

The learner performs with clear constraints and visible success criteria

### Variation and tradeoffs

Introduce cases where the same rule cannot be applied mechanically
Teach judgement and competing priorities

### Diagnosis and recovery

The learner finds why a result failed and repairs it

### Integrated real performance

The learner handles a realistic input case interaction task or problem through completion correction escalation delivery or handoff as promised

### Peak

The final integrated performance requires synthesis and independent decisions
Its compact formal proof occurs in the last project before the final module
The final module may analyse refine transfer package communicate deliver or close that performance but must not postpone a promise critical first introduction until after the last formal proof
The integrated project must not be a larger copy of the first exercise or an oversized capstone

  learner_project_effort_minutes:
  compression_findings: []

Approximately 120 spoken minutes is the default planning anchor for a standard course rather than a quota
The map may be shorter when a narrow promise is genuinely complete
It may be longer when the capability graph real performance episode workflow demonstrations cases diagnosis correction and completion require more time

Every standalone teaching reel must be between one and five spoken minutes after visible demonstration pauses are counted
Use word count only as a rough proxy
A screen action can consume time without spoken words

There is no default reel count ceiling
A high count including a count near 180 is valid only when each reel contains one distinct necessary capability change and the total map remains free of fragments repetition and filler
Do not compress a real course into thirty short reels or roughly forty minutes when that leaves the learner with general awareness rather than executable ability

When duration becomes excessive

1. Remove semantic duplication
2. Remove nonessential lookup material and express any essential reference as a durable spoken decision rule
3. Merge inseparable shallow fragments
4. Remove scope outside the promise
5. Preserve foundations native execution interaction diagnosis correction transfer and any required client or delivery reality
6. Narrow the promise only when the required competence still cannot fit

Do not remove a required performance or workflow step merely to meet an attractive runtime
Do not add reels merely to reach the 120 minute anchor

## One reel one meaningful change

A reel must be narrow enough to hold one coherent change and deep enough to justify its existence

Merge reels when

- They produce the same learner decision
- One is only setup for the other
- Each would be shallow alone
- Their examples and explanations substantially overlap

Split a reel when

- It contains separate independent actions
- It combines a mental model and a long screen execution that need different delivery modes
- It requires multiple practice cycles
- One part can fail without the other

Do not split content to reach a lesson count
Do not merge content merely to appear concise

## Independent value and sequence value

Each reel should create a complete useful thought that can stand alone when shared
It must also occupy a necessary place in the sequence

A standalone reel still needs prior terms introduced within the reel or phrased accessibly
A sequential reel must not depend on phrases such as as we said without restating the minimum needed premise

Do not repeat a whole prior lesson in the opening to create continuity
Use a short causal dependency

The standalone payoff and the sequence need must both be true
A reel that only sets up the next reel is incomplete
A reel that gives value but could sit anywhere despite claiming a dependency has weak sequence architecture
A genuinely parallel lesson may close quietly without forcing a loop

## High signal mapping

At map stage label the source of value for every reel

- Counterintuitive correction
- Diagnostic test
- Decision rule
- Causal mental model
- Demonstrated transformation
- Failure analysis
- Tradeoff boundary
- Professional shortcut with conditions
- Comparison that changes choice
- Workflow step whose omission causes a material failure
- Adaptation to a real context
- Perceptual cue that changes action
- Interaction signal and response adjustment
- Teaching correction that changes the second attempt

If a lesson has no source of value it is probably filler or reference material
Reference material that does not change performance should be removed from the generated course rather than exported as a companion file

## Surface level detector

Reject or deepen a planned lesson when

- A person outside the field already knows the conclusion
- The title fully contains the lesson value
- The advice is true but gives no decision or execution method
- The lesson could apply unchanged to every profession
- It says quality clarity consistency testing research or strategy are important without showing how
- It offers a slogan where the learner needs a model
- It repeats common social media advice without adding conditions evidence or diagnosis

Deepen using one or more of

- Why the obvious advice fails
- What evidence changes the decision
- A before and after decision path
- A real constraint
- A counterexample
- A failure signature
- A boundary where the rule reverses
- A test the learner can run

## Real performance completeness

Convert one realistic first use episode into an exact action inventory
Use the course promise rather than a fixed business template

Inspect the map for these often omitted layers when relevant

- Authentic trigger request stimulus case or input
- Recognition listening retrieval or starting state diagnosis
- Clarifying goals meaning success criteria scope or missing information
- Asset data access permission material tool or rights collection
- Setup environment naming versioning record or information organisation
- Foundational controls and authority boundaries
- Planning sequencing and time cost pacing or risk judgement
- Execution interaction explanation teaching or creation sequence
- Quality inspection or comprehension check before completion
- Error misunderstanding failure diagnosis correction recovery or escalation
- Variations edge cases changed constraints accents counterpart behaviour or context
- Client learner customer stakeholder team system or environment response
- Feedback interpretation revision scope change second attempt approval or transfer check
- Packaging documentation closure delivery acceptance referral or handoff
- The first realistic independent supervised or professional context promised by the course

For a job oriented course include the complete work around the central craft
For language include comprehension production adaptation and closure rather than vocabulary recognition alone
For sales include discovery judgement objection adaptation documentation follow up and ethical boundaries rather than scripts alone
For call centre language include listening intent clarification case action notes escalation and closure rather than phrases alone
For teaching include model elicitation diagnosis correction and transfer rather than explanation alone
For knowledge include retrieval distinction misconception correction application and authority boundary rather than information exposure alone

Include only what the promise needs but never hide a required mode because the course title sounds simple
A pre existing supplied template or starter asset may support practice when it already exists before generation
It cannot replace teaching a perception decision phrase action failure check correction or recovery the learner must perform and the standard must not generate a companion brief to carry missing teaching

## No missing middle audit

Run a cold start walkthrough using only the course map the lecturer DOCX and any pre existing supplied assets
Place the learner at the first authentic input stimulus request case conversation teaching moment or action state and advance one step at a time until the promised completion correction escalation referral delivery or handoff

At every step ask

- Does the learner know what to notice hear retrieve or do now
- Do they know what information phrase asset access permission material or context is required
- Do they know how to choose when more than one path response or interpretation is plausible
- Do they know how to perform in the required language register pace or channel
- Do they know how to verify that the step understanding response output or state is correct
- Do they know what a common failure misunderstanding objection weak result or unsafe condition looks like and the smallest correction recovery or escalation
- Do they know what to communicate teach document record save name export close refer or hand off
- Has this been taught and practised before it is required

Record every gap in `WORKFLOW_COVERAGE_MATRIX`
Add teaching demonstration practice a compact project or an explicit pre existing supplied asset
If the gap is outside scope narrow the promise and the simulated task so the learner never depends on it
Do not bridge a missing step with phrases equivalent to then respond naturally explain it clearly do the usual setup prepare the files handle the client teach the student or deliver professionally

## Map compression

After the first complete map

1. Compare outcomes not titles
2. Merge semantic duplicates
3. Remove motivational and logistical lessons
4. Remove volatile spoken details and replace them with durable verification methods
5. Remove adjacent specialisations outside the promise
6. Preserve foundations performance glue perception retrieval execution interaction teaching diagnosis correction completion escalation and delivery
7. Recheck the capability and workflow coverage matrices
8. Recalculate the duration budget and reel boundaries

Compression must increase signal density without deleting competence

## Map stress tests

Test the map using at least these scenarios

- A learner with zero formal background
- A learner with scattered online exposure
- A realistic first independent supervised paid professional or applied performance episode
- A changed input counterpart accent learner response customer behaviour or environmental condition
- A low budget or limited access tool setup
- A result that looks acceptable but is actually weak
- A common failure requiring diagnosis
- Missing access data context phrase asset permission counterpart response or client answer at the expected step
- A misunderstanding objection teaching error or interaction that requires adaptation
- Feedback that conflicts with the original brief expands scope or reveals non transfer
- A rejected delivery export failure or requested revision
- A changed tool or platform
- A local market context different from the source examples

The map must explain how each learner progresses and how each scenario is handled

## Architecture blockers

Block writing when

- The final integrated performance does not require most core capabilities
- More than one lesson has substantially the same outcome
- The first useful execution happens too late
- The course teaches creation but not inspection or correction
- The course teaches theory but not retrieval distinction application or performance
- The course teaches language but not listening retrieval production correction and transfer
- The course teaches live interaction through memorised scripts without response adaptation
- The course teaches teaching through explanation without elicitation diagnosis correction and transfer

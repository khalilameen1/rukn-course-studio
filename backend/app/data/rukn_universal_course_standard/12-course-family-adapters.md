# 12 UNIVERSAL CAPABILITY COMPOSITION

## Universal rule

Do not decide course architecture from the title category industry or a single course family label

Every course is a composition of observable capability units
A course that appears to belong to one field may still require several learning modes
Treat mixed composition as the default rather than the exception

Named modes in this file are reusable defaults
They are not an exhaustive taxonomy and they are not module templates
When a required capability does not fit them cleanly derive a bounded custom capability mode from the real performance instead of forcing a weak match

Store the complete composition in

- `COURSE_PERFORMANCE_COMPOSITION`
- `PERFORMANCE_EPISODE_MAP`
- `CAPABILITY_ADAPTER_MAP`
- `CAPABILITY_MODE_MATRIX`
- `CUSTOM_ADAPTER_REGISTRY`
- `PEDAGOGY_ADAPTER`

`CAPABILITY_ADAPTER_MAP` is the canonical capability level assignment
`CAPABILITY_MODE_MATRIX` is its coverage and validation view
In this file mode names the reusable performance pattern and adapter names its accepted application to a capability
`PEDAGOGY_ADAPTER` is retained as a compatibility object and must point to the complete capability composition rather than naming one adapter for the whole course

The universal standard controls truth depth progression language projects evergreen durability and output purity
Capability modes control how a specific capability is modelled practised corrected varied and proved
Context overlays control additional source safety realism and delivery requirements

Do not expose mode names matrices or overlays in the lecturer DOCX

## Universal learning cycle

For every important capability provide the smallest complete path from first contact to independent use

1. Establish the real situation goal and quality standard
2. Model or demonstrate a valid performance decision or result
3. Explain the causal reason pattern or decision rule
4. Give a constrained learner attempt
5. Expose an observable signal of strength or weakness
6. Diagnose the error misunderstanding or failed choice
7. Correct and repeat
8. Change one meaningful condition
9. Require a less supported or independent attempt
10. Connect the capability to its real handoff boundary or next dependency

The cycle may span several reels and practices
Do not force every step into one reel
Do not count explanation recognition copying or watching as independent performance

A low risk simple capability may need fewer repetitions
A high risk live ambiguous or highly transferable capability needs corrected retries and changed contexts

## Mode selection discipline

Select a mode from the learner action and proof required
Do not select it from the subject noun

Examples

- Explaining a rule accurately is a knowledge authority capability
- Using that rule while teaching a confused learner is a teaching facilitation capability supported by knowledge authority and live communication
- Memorising call centre phrases is language exposure
- Handling a caller who changes the problem requires language live communication operational workflow and diagnosis
- Reading sales metrics is analytical
- conducting discovery is live communication
- recording the outcome and next step is operational

When two modes appear equally central ask which failure would make the completed capability invalid
Use that as the primary mode and retain the other as secondary

Do not average modes into generic explanation practice and project language
Each capability keeps the native learning and evidence requirements of its modes

## Perceptual discrimination and quality inspection mode

Use when the learner must notice hear see feel compare classify or inspect signals whose differences control later action
This mode may be a complete capability or a supporting mode inside language design quality control teaching technical diagnosis physical performance and other fields

Mandatory capability loop

1. Establish the target signal quality standard and decision consequence
2. Contrast clear positive negative and near miss examples
3. Direct attention to the smallest reliable cues
4. Classify or detect with visible reasoning where appropriate
5. Distinguish the target under changed speaker material environment scale or noise
6. Identify what the signal proves and what it does not prove
7. Link the observation to the correct action correction or escalation
8. Repeat until the learner can discriminate without answer cues

Mandatory evidence

- Representative inputs or samples
- Contrasts including near misses
- Learner classification detection or inspection result
- Cue based reason when the capability requires explainable judgement
- Changed condition attempt
- Correct action correction or escalation linked to the signal

Progression pressure may grow through weaker cues closer alternatives noisy inputs changed accents speakers materials lighting scale speed context or several plausible causes

Do not count naming the rule as proof of perception
Do not count one memorised example as transfer
Do not ask for verbal reasoning when expert performance is legitimately rapid and tacit unless explanation is part of the promised role
When perception supports another capability ensure later practice requires the learner to act on what was detected

## Production and creative mode

Use when the learner must create or revise an artefact such as a design video article product page photograph edited asset presentation or written deliverable

Mandatory capability loop

1. Interpret the brief audience purpose and constraints
2. Select a direction for a reason
3. Build an editable first version
4. Inspect against functional evidence
5. Diagnose weakness
6. Revise without destroying strong parts
7. Adapt to another context format or constraint
8. Package and deliver professionally

Mandatory evidence

- Source or starter state
- Decision record where judgement is material
- Work in progress when process structure matters
- First version
- Critique or inspection evidence
- Revised version
- Editable source or delivery output when promised

Progression pressure may grow through ambiguity competing constraints revision pressure format changes stakeholder feedback or delivery requirements

Do not judge only visual stylistic or personal taste
Do not let tool fluency substitute for decision quality

## Operational process and service workflow mode

Use when the learner must run a repeatable process coordinate states monitor progress follow policy recover from exceptions or hand work to another person or system

Mandatory capability loop

1. Identify the trigger goal scope and current state
2. Gather required inputs access and permissions
3. Choose or confirm the correct process path
4. Execute the next valid action
5. Record or communicate the state change
6. Check completion and quality signals
7. Detect delay exception or mismatch
8. Recover escalate or reroute
9. Close hand off archive or schedule the next action

Mandatory evidence

- Starting condition
- Process or case path
- Executed state change
- Check or confirmation
- Exception decision
- Recovery or escalation
- Handoff or closure record

Progression pressure may grow through incomplete inputs multiple cases competing priorities exceptions policy boundaries time pressure or handoff dependencies

Do not reduce an operational skill to a checklist read aloud
Do not teach only the happy path
Do not assume access setup documentation follow up or escalation happens automatically

## Operational marketing and media mode

Use when the learner runs a process whose result appears through later signals and decisions

Mandatory capability loop

1. Define the business or operational objective
2. Separate known facts assumptions and unknowns
3. Choose a hypothesis and measurement signal
4. Configure or execute the action
5. Check implementation quality
6. Read early and mature signals without overclaiming
7. Diagnose the likely cause
8. Decide what to keep change stop or investigate
9. Communicate the decision and uncertainty

Mandatory evidence

- Brief and constraints
- Decision rationale
- Setup campaign or content structure
- Measurement plan
- Simulated or real signal set
- Diagnosis
- Next decision

Progression pressure may grow through noisier signals delayed outcomes conflicting indicators budget constraints stakeholder expectations and uncertain causality

Never invent performance numbers
Never teach that one metric proves causality by itself
Use simulated datasets when real spend is unavailable and label them internally
Use the visible interface only as an implementation example while the spoken script teaches the transferable decision and avoids fragile current paths limits or plan details

## Sales service and live communication mode

Use when success depends on listening responding persuading supporting negotiating de escalating interviewing or coordinating with another person in real time or near real time

Mandatory capability loop

1. Read the situation role relationship and goal
2. Listen gather information and verify meaning
3. Identify the response function and decision boundary
4. Perform naturally rather than recite
5. Notice the other party signal
6. Adapt the content tone pace question or next action
7. Handle objection misunderstanding emotion or changed information
8. Confirm agreement understanding ownership or next step
9. Close or hand off appropriately

Mandatory practice

- Model performance
- Controlled response
- Listening or information gathering
- Branching scenarios
- Objection misunderstanding or emotional variation
- Timed or live attempt where relevant
- Targeted feedback
- Corrected second attempt
- Changed context transfer

Mandatory evidence

- Spoken or written interaction performance native to the job
- Response to at least one changed signal
- Evidence of clarification diagnosis or adaptation
- Appropriate close handoff or next step

Progression pressure may grow through ambiguity pace emotional intensity incomplete information competing goals difficult objections multiple stakeholders or reduced preparation time

Do not turn the course into memorised scripts only
Reusable phrases support judgement but do not replace it
Do not count a written script as proof of live delivery listening timing or adaptation

## Language use mode

Use when the target capability includes comprehension speech writing pronunciation register or interaction in another language

Define functions rather than topic vocabulary alone

Examples of functions include requesting clarification explaining a problem managing a lesson verifying identity negotiating scope responding to feedback calming a caller documenting an outcome or closing an interaction

Mandatory learning loop

1. Understand meaning purpose and context
2. Notice form pronunciation register and likely response
3. Retrieve without seeing the answer
4. Use in controlled variation
5. Understand a realistic incoming message or utterance
6. Produce a response in a realistic interaction
7. Receive specific correction
8. Repeat with changed wording speaker condition or context
9. Recycle after spacing

Mandatory evidence

- Comprehension check
- Spoken or written production
- Pronunciation fluency or register target where relevant
- Error diagnosis
- Corrected second performance
- Transfer to a new scenario

Progression pressure may grow through less preparation faster retrieval unfamiliar wording accent or audio variation longer turns changed intent professional register and spontaneous interaction

Do not let explanation in the learner language consume most practice time
Do not teach isolated lists without retrieval comprehension and use
Do not call recognition speaking ability
Keep target language phrases exact natural and appropriate to the real role
When the course is language for work combine this mode with the relevant job workflow interaction knowledge and tool modes
Language accuracy alone does not prove job performance

## Teaching facilitation and coaching mode

Use when the learner must cause another person to understand practise improve decide or perform rather than merely present information

Mandatory capability loop

1. Define the learner outcome and evidence of learning
2. Check prior state attention language and likely misconception
3. Select the smallest useful explanation example or demonstration
4. Model the target performance accurately
5. Prompt an observable learner response
6. Notice what the response proves and does not prove
7. Diagnose the misunderstanding or performance error
8. Choose one proportionate correction
9. Require a corrected attempt
10. Change the example condition or prompt to test transfer
11. Reinforce the right element and set the next practice boundary

Mandatory evidence

- Bounded lesson or interaction objective
- Clear explanation or demonstration
- Learner prompt
- Plausible learner response or branch
- Error diagnosis
- Specific correction
- Corrected second attempt
- Transfer check or follow up action

Progression pressure may grow through learner variation hidden misunderstanding language difference limited attention repeated error mixed ability emotional resistance or the need to adapt without losing the objective

Do not count a lesson plan as proof of teaching performance
Do not count fluent explanation as proof that learning occurred
Do not teach correction as a list of generic encouragement phrases
For subject specific teaching combine this mode with the relevant knowledge authority language live communication physical or technical modes

## Programming technical and no code mode

Use when the learner builds operates configures debugs integrates secures or maintains a technical system

Mandatory capability loop

1. Explain the system model environment and state flow
2. Build or configure the smallest working path
3. Observe actual behaviour
4. Introduce realistic complexity
5. Trigger or inspect a failure
6. Diagnose with evidence
7. Fix and test
8. Refactor organise or document for maintainability
9. Secure configure and deploy within scope
10. Document handoff recovery and known boundaries

Mandatory evidence

- Reproducible environment or setup
- Working artefact or configuration
- Failing case
- Diagnostic evidence
- Passing tests or verification
- Versioned source or inspectable structure
- Deployment package configuration or handoff when promised

Progression pressure may grow through interacting components hidden state unreliable inputs permissions security deployment constraints and failures with several plausible causes

Do not read code or interface labels aloud line by line
Explain state flow assumptions tradeoffs and failure evidence
Verify current library and platform behaviour internally then teach the durable system model test and verification method without recording short lived version claims
Do not expose credentials or teach insecure shortcuts as production practice

## Analytical data and decision mode

Use when the learner evaluates evidence estimates forecasts prices diagnoses prioritises compares options or makes a bounded recommendation under uncertainty

Mandatory capability loop

1. Define the decision question and decision owner
2. Audit the available evidence data scope and units
3. Separate fact assumption inference and estimate
4. Choose a suitable method benchmark or comparison
5. Calculate analyse or reason with an audit trail
6. Test alternative explanations or assumptions
7. Interpret uncertainty and decision risk
8. Choose the next action recommendation or information need
9. Communicate the conclusion and boundary

Mandatory evidence

- Source data or case evidence
- Unit scope and relevance checks
- Assumption ledger
- Reproducible method
- Sensitivity alternative case or competing hypothesis
- Decision with confidence boundary

Progression pressure may grow through missing data noisy evidence conflicting goals uncertain causality larger consequence and the need to communicate a decision without false certainty

Never manufacture a missing price quantity statistic source value or current market rate
Do not present model output as source evidence
Use escalation or human approval where the decision has material commercial safety legal medical or financial consequence

## Knowledge authority and interpretation mode

Use when the learner must retrieve explain distinguish classify interpret or apply a body of knowledge accurately within a stated boundary

Mandatory capability loop

1. Establish source authority context and terms
2. Understand the claim distinction or rule
3. Separate source text interpretation opinion convention and application
4. Compare correct and incorrect examples
5. Retrieve explain classify or justify without copying
6. Correct a real misunderstanding
7. Apply to a bounded case
8. State what cannot be inferred and when qualified referral is required

Mandatory evidence

- Accurate source use
- Contextual explanation
- Distinction or classification
- Misconception diagnosis
- Retrieval or explanation task
- Bounded application case

Progression pressure may grow through near distinctions conflicting interpretations incomplete cases exceptions misleading surface similarity and stronger source discipline

Do not force portfolio language business framing or artificial production projects onto this mode
Use explanation classification recitation analysis teaching simulation or case judgement according to the promise
For religious content preserve the requested etiquette terminology authority and scope boundaries without mixing ceremonial formal language into otherwise natural speech in a way that sounds artificial
Never fabricate a quotation attribution consensus ruling or personal spiritual experience

## Physical embodied and equipment performance mode

Use when the learner performs movement craft equipment handling voice production manual procedure or another embodied action

Mandatory capability loop

1. Establish safety environment and start position
2. Observe the full movement or process
3. Identify critical components and sensory signals
4. Perform slowly with feedback
5. Detect unsafe ineffective or inaccurate form
6. Correct one variable
7. Repeat under realistic speed load sequence or context
8. Maintain quality across variation
9. Know stop recovery supervision and escalation conditions

Mandatory evidence

- Clear demonstration angles or sound where relevant
- Safety boundaries
- Observable form or process criteria
- First attempt
- Specific correction
- Second attempt
- Transfer under a bounded changed condition

Progression pressure may grow through coordination speed load precision fatigue environmental variation equipment state and reduced supervision

Spoken explanation cannot replace visual or auditory demonstration and supervised practice where safety requires it
A static upload cannot prove timing force coordination movement or live voice performance

## Context overlays

Apply overlays after primary and secondary modes are selected
An overlay modifies research practice boundaries and proof without becoming a generic module

### High consequence regulated or safety overlay

Require authoritative current sources scope boundaries prohibited actions stop conditions escalation and human approval where appropriate
Use conservative claims and never imply that course completion replaces a licence supervised practice diagnosis legal advice medical judgement or another regulated authority

### Live timed interaction overlay

Require realistic incoming signals listening pace turn taking interruption recovery and at least one performance with reduced preparation
A prepared document cannot replace live evidence

### Tool or platform mediated overlay

Require setup access state changes verification failure recovery data or file hygiene and handoff
Teach durable system logic and live verification rather than fragile interface coordinates current plan limits or temporary pricing

### Client stakeholder or team overlay

Require brief clarification expectation setting communication approval feedback revision scope boundary and handoff only where the promised role actually includes them
Do not add freelancing business content to a narrow employee task unless needed

### Multilingual or cross cultural overlay

Require meaning register politeness relationship expectations likely transfer errors and context appropriate phrasing
Do not translate word for word when the professional function changes

### Remote asynchronous overlay

Require written clarity delayed feedback evidence capture version state follow up and escalation without assuming immediate verbal repair

### Accessibility or constrained access overlay

Require viable methods for the learner device budget bandwidth ability and tool access constraints
Do not silently make essential practice depend on an inaccessible paid environment

### Evergreen volatility overlay

Identify facts values interfaces rankings fees plans laws and market conditions likely to change
Verify them internally when needed then teach a durable decision method source checking habit or clearly hypothetical example
Do not freeze volatile values into recorded lecturer speech

## Composition examples

Use these only to verify reasoning
Never copy their structure without rebuilding the target course from its own promise workflow sources and audience

### Sales performance

Likely capability composition

- Discovery and need clarification uses sales service and live communication as primary with analytical judgement as secondary
- Offer selection uses analytical decision as primary with knowledge authority and operational constraints as secondary
- Objection handling uses live communication as primary with knowledge and language support
- Follow up recording and handoff uses operational process as primary
- A sales language course adds language use to every capability where the learner must comprehend or produce the target language

A course that teaches persuasive phrases but omits discovery adaptation qualification follow up and handoff fails the real performance

### Language for a call centre or customer support role

Likely capability composition

- Understanding the caller and verifying meaning uses language use with live communication
- Diagnosing the request uses operational workflow and analytical judgement
- Explaining a solution uses language use knowledge authority and live communication
- Handling frustration uses live communication with language register and service boundaries
- Using hold transfer escalation documentation and closure uses operational workflow and tool mediated overlays
- Privacy identity payment or regulated cases activate the high consequence overlay when relevant

A phrase catalogue or grammar course cannot substitute for listening branch decisions tool state documentation recovery and handoff

### Teaching a subject to learners in another language

Likely capability composition

- Subject accuracy uses knowledge authority
- Classroom language comprehension and production uses language use
- Explanation modelling elicitation diagnosis correction and transfer use teaching facilitation
- Learner response adaptation uses live communication
- Online lesson setup files or platform actions use operational or technical modes where required

A translated subject summary or vocabulary list cannot substitute for teaching performance

### Design or content production

Likely capability composition

- Brief interpretation and direction selection combine production analytical judgement and stakeholder context
- Tool execution uses production with technical support
- Critique and revision use production and analytical diagnosis
- Feedback scope delivery files and handoff use operational workflow and stakeholder overlays

A button tour or attractive final image alone cannot prove professional delivery capability

## Project submission forms by mode

Every standard non final module project must end in an upload form native to the dominant exit capability and supported by the platform
The final boundary project must use the smallest attachment set native to its connected promise critical mode combination
Select the smallest image screenshot document source file audio video data file or other allowed file that makes the learner result directly inspectable
Run the internal attachment self evidence check before accepting the form
This section designs learner evidence only
It stops when the task attachment form and visible result are clear and never expands into evaluator scoring feedback or post upload logic

### Perceptual discrimination and quality inspection

Use a short document image annotated screenshot audio response video response or structured file that contains the supplied samples learner classifications detected cues and the resulting action required by the project
The evidence must include changed or near miss examples when transfer matters
Do not accept a copied label list as proof of discrimination

Prefer one image screenshot exported artefact or editable source file that exposes the required decision and result
Request a source file only when structure editability layers timing or another internal property is itself the capability
Do not require a full portfolio case for an inter module project

### Sales service and live communication

Mixed composition is normal
Modules are grouped by dependency real workflow and learner readiness rather than by mode labels


Rules

- Do not create one module per mode unless the real workflow and dependencies genuinely require it
- Do not average modes into generic teaching
- Do not let the most visible mode erase operational setup diagnosis correction documentation or handoff
- Do not let language support replace job competence or let job competence excuse weak language practice
- Do not let subject expertise replace teaching ability or let teaching technique excuse inaccurate content
- Do not let a tool demonstration replace judgement interaction or recovery
- Build the final integrated performance from the actual mode combination required by the promise
- Place its compact formal proof in the last project before the final module and include every promise critical mode
- Use the final module to diagnose refine transfer package communicate deliver hand off or close already practised integrated performance rather than introducing a new promise critical standalone mode
- Build module bridges from dependencies between owned capabilities and newly exposed real conditions rather than from topic labels

## Domain neutrality stress test

Before accepting architecture test the exact course against these questions

- Was every promised real situation decomposed into episodes rather than topic headings
- Does every required capability have one defensible primary mode secondary modes where needed and active overlays
- Would changing the course title while preserving the same performance leave the mode assignments substantially unchanged
- Does every required perception listening reading visual or quality discrimination capability receive contrast near miss and changed condition practice rather than explanation only
- Does a language for work course teach language comprehension production and correction inside the actual job workflow
- Does a sales service or support course include listening branch decisions adaptation documentation closure and handoff rather than scripts only
- Does a teaching course include learner evidence diagnosis correction retry and transfer rather than explanation only
- Does a production course include brief judgement inspection revision delivery and file or state hygiene rather than tool steps only
- Does a knowledge course use retrieval distinction correction and bounded application without artificial portfolio conventions
- Does a technical course include failure diagnosis testing recovery and maintainable handoff rather than a clean build only
- Does every project use evidence native to its dominant capability and pass attachment self evidence inspectability
- Does the last boundary project integrate every promise critical capability while remaining compact
- Does the final module resolve already practised performance without introducing a new promise critical standalone capability
- Is any capability being forced into a mode merely because the domain is unfamiliar
- If a required capability fits no mode was a valid custom mode generated and stress tested

Failure on any material question reopens composition before module mapping

## Composition gate

Block architecture when

- One adapter is applied to the entire course without capability level decomposition
- `COURSE_PERFORMANCE_COMPOSITION` `PERFORMANCE_EPISODE_MAP` `CAPABILITY_ADAPTER_MAP` or `CAPABILITY_MODE_MATRIX` is missing or unresolved
- The selected mode follows the course title rather than the observable end performance
- A required performance episode capability mode or context overlay is missing
- The primary mode cannot explain where independence judgement failure correction and proof live
- Secondary modes required for real work were discarded for simplicity
- Practice evidence comes from another mode and cannot prove the skill
- A mixed course uses one teaching treatment project shape or attachment type for every capability
- A non final module exit has no platform supported upload form that can make the completed capability directly inspectable
- An attachment self evidence check is missing fails or has expanded into evaluator scoring feedback or post upload logic
- The last boundary project omits a promise critical mode or the final module introduces one after formal proof has ended
- The selected upload shows only appearance while the capability depends on structure process runtime interaction listening adaptation teaching diagnosis or physical performance
- Explanation is counted as performance
- Rule recall is counted as perception discrimination or quality inspection
- Recognition is counted as production or live language ability
- A lesson plan is counted as teaching performance
- A memorised script is counted as sales service or call handling ability
- Tool completion is counted as professional judgement
- A simulated result is presented as real
- A knowledge course is forced into irrelevant career or portfolio conventions
- An enumerated mode is stretched beyond fit instead of generating a bounded custom mode
- A custom mode has no performance loop native evidence correction pattern or source requirement

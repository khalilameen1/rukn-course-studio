# 02 RESEARCH MARKET AND VOICE CALIBRATION

## Separate evidence roles

Every source receives one or more explicit roles
Never let a source acquire authority merely because it is long polished or similar in tone

### FACT AUTHORITY

Used for factual technical legal theological medical financial platform or product claims

Prefer in order when applicable

1. Primary texts standards laws and official documentation
2. Original research datasets and first party records
3. Recognised professional or academic references
4. Current experienced practitioner evidence
5. High quality secondary explanation

### MARKET EVIDENCE

Used to understand actual offers constraints language workflows expectations and buyer behaviour in the target market
Use current local examples and multiple signals
Do not infer performance from mere public existence
Do not treat the loudest local style as the only valid local style

### PRACTITIONER REALITY

Used for failure modes tradeoffs workflow friction cost access and what happens after a tutorial ends
Prefer detailed postmortems case studies and experienced practitioner discussions over promotional tool copy

### VOICE REFERENCE

Used only to calibrate human rhythm explanation density sentence construction transition behaviour and domain native speech
It is not factual authority unless independently qualified

### FLOW REFERENCE

Used to study how a real instructor carries a long explanation
It may be an imperfect transcript
Never copy its wording catchphrases errors or episode structure
Never import its presenter persona reaction style artificial suspense editing rhythm or entertainment mannerisms

### EXERCISE OR CASE MATERIAL

Used as raw material for a learning task
Track rights privacy and whether the case is real synthetic or adapted

## Source firewall

Maintain these separations

- Facts come from fact authority
- Local assumptions come from market evidence
- Voice comes from voice references
- Teaching sequence comes from the capability graph
- Course promise comes from the intake and thesis
- Examples must be verified or clearly synthetic

A transcript cannot establish a technical fact because the speaker sounds credible
Official documentation cannot dictate natural spoken delivery
Social posts cannot define the full curriculum
Vendor marketing cannot define realistic capability or limitations

## Voice calibration depth

When a suitable reference creator exists and sufficient material is available review at least 20000 words before extracting a voice profile

A suitable main reference has

- Abundant authentic material
- Sustained explanation rather than isolated captions
- A language variety compatible with the target
- Credible domain fluency or a transferable explanatory style
- Enough variation to distinguish natural habits from one episode choices

If 20000 usable words are unavailable do not pretend to have deep calibration
Use multiple secondary references or a neutral natural practitioner voice

Extract abstract properties only

- Average thought length
- How context is introduced
- How examples enter
- How terms are explained
- How objections are anticipated
- How transitions feel
- How confidence and uncertainty are expressed
- How dense ideas remain understandable
- Which connectors command forms question forms number forms and sentence structures belong to the requested spoken register
- Which otherwise correct forms sound written formal translated or foreign to that register

Do not extract

- Signature lines
- Repeated catchphrases
- Famous openings
- Personal anecdotes
- Identifiable metaphors
- Recurrent templates
- Presenter theatrics shouting reactions urgency or suspense devices
- Word for word sentences

## Imperfect transcript handling

Assume automated transcripts may contain

- Wrong Arabic words
- Lost English terms
- Incorrect speaker boundaries
- Missing punctuation
- Repeated segments
- Phonetic guesses
- Broken sentence boundaries

Use an imperfect transcript to infer broad flow and human teaching behaviour
Do not adopt a strange word merely because it appears in the transcript
Resolve domain terms from independent practitioner usage
Do not build a spoken residue policy from one automated transcript
Confirm register patterns through multiple clear authentic recordings or reliable native practitioner evidence
Discard unclear passages rather than completing them from imagination

## Market calibration

Build `MARKET_PACK` before mapping market dependent skills

```yaml
market_pack:
  geography:
  learner_economics:
  common_client_or_employer_types: []
  common_work_contexts: []
  accessible_tools: []
  professional_minimum_tools: []
  pricing_or_budget_realities: []
  language_and_term_usage: []
  spoken_register_evidence: []
  written_register_residue_candidates: []
  platform_and_device_realities: []
  common_bad_practices: []
  imported_assumptions_to_reject: []
  local_cliches_to_reject: []
  current_requirements_to_verify: []
```

For Egypt or another non Western target market

- Do not import Western budgets workflows aesthetics labour assumptions or buying behaviour by default
- Do not localise by adding superficial place names slang or stereotyped clutter
- Use local businesses devices payment realities client behaviour and access constraints when they change the decision
- Preserve globally valid professional principles
- Distinguish local convention from universal truth

The goal is globally strong judgement operating in the local reality
It is not Western content translated into Arabic
It is not weak local convention presented as professional truth

## Competitive learning benchmark

Build `BENCHMARK_MATRIX` for the exact promise rather than the broad topic

Inspect strong current alternatives that are legitimately accessible for analysis

- Authoritative curricula and certification outlines
- Highly regarded practical courses
- Strong free educational series
- Professional competency frameworks
- Current employer client or practitioner expectations

Use public outlines previews documentation independent reviews and lawfully supplied materials
Do not infer lesson quality from popularity price production value or marketing claims
Do not copy protected lesson text examples projects or structure

Record

```yaml
- benchmark:
  name:
  market:
  evidence_available:
  stated_outcome:
  capabilities_covered: []
  practical_evidence: []
  apparent_strengths: []
  unverified_claims: []
  gaps_relevant_to_our_promise: []
```

Then compare the proposed course

- Does it omit a capability repeatedly treated as essential by strong authorities
- Does it explain mechanisms or only provide steps
- Does it include execution diagnosis revision and delivery
- Does it offer credible practice and evidence of readiness
- Does it adapt globally strong principles to the target market
- Is any claimed advantage real and demonstrable

Benchmarking is a gap detector not an imitation instruction
The course must earn its own architecture from its thesis and capability graph

## Current and evergreen evidence

Classify every important claim

- Stable principle
- Slowly changing professional convention
- Current market condition
- Current tool capability
- Current interface or platform specification
- Legal or policy requirement

For unstable claims verify close to generation time
Record verification date and authority internally
Teach the principle and the method for checking the current value when the value is likely to change

Do not fill an evergreen course with transient numbers interface coordinates or vendor rankings
Use a current tool as an example only when the learner can transfer the method to a replacement

## Claim ledger

Record material claims with

```yaml
- claim:
  claim_type:
  source_role:
  authority:
  geography:
  verified_at:
  confidence:
  course_locations: []
  update_trigger:
```

No material current claim enters the script without ledger support
The final teleprompter does not need to speak citations unless the subject or user requires them

## Contradiction handling

When sources disagree

1. Check whether they address different versions markets contexts or definitions
2. Prefer primary and current authority for facts
3. Preserve real professional uncertainty when no answer dominates
4. Teach the decision boundary instead of forcing a false universal rule
5. Do not average incompatible claims

## Rights privacy and provenance

Track whether source material may be quoted reused transformed or shown
Default to synthesis rather than copying
Do not expose private client data personal data credentials or confidential business material
Synthetic cases must be labelled internally and must not claim invented results

## Research completeness gate

Research is complete only when it can support

- The course promise
- Every foundational mental model
- Every material workflow
- Common failure diagnosis
- Market adaptation
- Professional delivery
- Current specifications that affect execution
- Terminology used in speech
- Project realism
- A competitive coverage check for the exact promised outcome

More sources do not equal better research
Remove low authority repetitive promotional and irrelevant material from the active context
